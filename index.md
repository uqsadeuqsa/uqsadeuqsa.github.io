# Uqsa de Uqsa

The Uqsa de Uqsa (pronounced: /ʔuqṣɑ ðəʔuqṣɑ/) music project creates music featuring vocals in **ancient Aramaic**. The singing style, primarily **rap**, closely approximates original pronunciation. It's energetic, raw, and intense, reminiscent of **Eminem's** powerful delivery.

The lyrics are written in the **Hebrew alphabet, fully vocalized** using the standard Hebrew diacritical system. They are accompanied by faithful Hebrew and English translations (with inter-word clarifications), rendered as literally as possible while conveying the intended meaning.

The lyrics use the three specific **dead Aramaic dialects** used in Hebrew scriptures—**Old Aramaic** (as found in the books of Ezra and Daniel), **Jewish Babylonian Aramaic** (a later eastern dialect, as found in the Talmud), and **Palestinian Jewish Aramaic** (a western dialect, as found in Onkelos translations and the Book of Zohar). The main dialect is the Babylonian one, mixing the other two frequently, even in the same sentence (some of the scriptures also do that, mix those dialects). The careful selection of vocabulary ensures the **absence of profanity, allusions to sex or immorality.**

Uqsa de Uqsa is **entirely self-produced**, it relies solely on invested time, effort, and professional skills; no funds are dedicated to advertising or external services. All aspects of production—from music creation and lyric writing to video production and marketing—are handled solely by the project's creator—a vocal performer, IT engineer, polyglot, composer, and multi-instrumentalist. He is also an expert on Hebrew scriptures and ancient Aramaic, speaking these dialects fluently.

## Persona

The project's persona is "**Uqsa de Uqsa**," a **Middle Eastern rapper of Western descent**. This unique background informs his music, allowing him to blend cultural influences in a way that challenges stereotypes within the rap genre. Uqsa embodies a **strong moral compass**, delivering powerful condemnations of wrongdoing while offering unwavering support for those who strive for righteousness. His delivery is intense and raw, yet it serves a clear purpose: to highlight the consequences of evil without directing harshness at the innocent.

The content is suitable for mature audiences — the content is wholesome in intent, though some tracks contain intense scriptural imagery and mature themes not suitable for small children (these tracks are marked with ⚠️).

The name "**Uqsa de Uqsa**" is an ironic juxtaposition, mirroring the structure of *"šufra de šufra"* ("the best of the best") but substituting "*uqsa*" ("sting" or "thorn"). While *"šufra de šufra"* implies the highest quality, **"uqsa de uqsa" signifies the worst of the worst—intense negativity—reserved exclusively for the wicked**. This ironic naming highlights the project's themes and aggressive delivery, while underscoring the persona's unwavering moral clarity. The persona doesn't offer indiscriminate positivity; it delivers brutal truth only to those who deserve it. The name itself reflects the music's content and style.

Uqsa speaks in the first person, addressing "those bad people/things" in the third person, and "you bad person" or "ye bad people" in the second person (Aramaic distinguishes between 2nd person singular and plural).

## Music

The music spans a range of styles, including **rap, hiphop and their sub-genres (drill, ska, etc), mixing elements of rock, metal, and electronic**, with surprising elements of **authentic and traditional Middle-Eastern and North-African music** (distinct from what is commonly called 'Orientalist' music, that present exoticized and inaccurate characteristics of this music), at all maintaining a strong and consistent personal style and presentation across all genres. It strives to professionalism and virtuosity in all domains: instrument playing, lyrical and vocal abilities, etc.

Uqsa's music blends a free-flowing, free-style delivery with lyrical content, with occasionally hinting at, but not directly narrating, stories from scriptures.

The lyrics are mostly about **confronting injustice, exposing lies or mis-interpretations, and threatening the wrongdoers** of their own misfortune/punishment. Crucially, Uqsa's harshness is **never directed at the innocent**. His pronouncements are a stark condemnation of wrongdoing, balanced by unwavering positivity and encouragement for the righteous.

* **Moral Message Seekers**: Audiences valuing strong ethical themes, including certain religious communities.
* **Academic Communities**: Scholars of Aramaic and related fields.
* **Conceptual Art Fans**: Those drawn to projects with a strong artistic vision.
