# Man Hu Da?

⚠️ not for small children

Uqsa demands to be known: not a quiet whisper of conscience but a wasp that hunts openly, strikes repeatedly, and exposes evil in full public view. The song turns on a bleak, grinding image — the wicked descending step by step, deaf to every shouted warning — and contrasts it with the lies they write about themselves: official-looking documents of uprightness that are really full of slaughter and deceit. Judgment, when it comes, is visual and brutal. And the final word is Uqsa's grinning promise: one by one, he'll remove every last one of them. A manifesto delivered at full volume.

### Who is this?

⚠️ not for small children

> Did ye know who comes now?!  
> Who is this? What is this?  
> Where did he come from? And why is he here?!  
> Ahh??

> Me?! My name is Uqsa,  
> For they say that I am like a wasp  
> that comes to strike all the wicked  
> and to bring out the bad in public.

> And they are descending, and going, and descending,  
> from step to step,  
> to the end of all steps.

> And I said to them:  
> Do not go there! Do not descend there!  
> And they were not listening,  
> because their ears were blocked up.

> And I said this, and I shouted this  
> again and again and again and again, three times a day  
> Ah hah!

> And now, hee hee, in these times  
> Uqsa brings the wicked to their judgment —  
> Ah hah!

> And before the judgments they are afraid:  
> that they will be hung on high pillars,  
> and their eyes will become foods  
> for all that fly in the skies.

> They are writing writings of uprightness,  
> that are full of slaughter and lies,  
> and they show a strength that does not exist,  
> and they force all the world with this.

> To mess with Uqsa — it is not worth it.  
> Lest he turn upon all of them with this:  
> and he will strike each and every one of them,  
> that he remove the wicked from among them all.

## Production

This track is a high-octane **"Ethno-Trap" fusion** that blends the gritty, bass-heavy energy of modern EDM with traditional Eastern aesthetics.
Clocking in at approximately **130 BPM**, the piece is built on a driving rhythmic foundation of deep kick drums and sharp snares typical of trap music. The "eastern" soul of the track comes from a synthesized lead melody—reminiscent of plucked instruments like an **Oud or Sitar**—that utilizes exotic scales and pitch-bending techniques to create a sleek, professional, and culturally resonant soundscape.
