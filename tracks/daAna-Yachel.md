# daAna Yachel

The meta-manifesto. Over a swaggering beat, Uqsa explains the whole project: I can sing this, put it in an MP3, you can watch me even when I’m not there — so comment, tell me it’s beautiful. A comedian’s dialogue breaks out: “But Uqsa, why so mean? We wear white, help the poor, save frogs!” He retorts: you save all the fish in the sea and put them right onto your plate. The track then drops the irony and delivers the core thesis: a person cannot be in truth if the mouth doesn’t match the heart, and until evil is gone from you, the Sting keeps stinging. A song about itself, its medium, and its unflinching moral demand.

### For I can

> For I can sing this song,  
> and put my voice inside an MP3,  
> And ye can hear this song  
> in your dwellings and in your houses at every time and time.

> Beautiful, is it not? And there is also a vision _(~video)_  
> that I place my face inside it,  
> And ye are able to see me singing,  
> even if I am not there at all.

---

> And it is appropriate for ye to write _(~comment)_ that ye love this,  
> and that this song is exceedingly, exceedingly beautiful —  
> For of all the songs that people sing,  
> a song from the Sting of Stings is among the first one _(~ancient ones)_.

> For every single word that is in this song  
> comes out and stings you in the arse,  
> and strikes you harder than goads,  
> for this is a sting that stings more than all stings.

---

> But Uqsa, why do you speak wicked things?  
> For we all stand in white robes and overalls,  
> helping the poor and saving the frogs,  
> and showing the world only good deeds.

> Ye want to save the world,  
> but ye do not succeed in loving one another,  
> and ye save all the fish in the sea  
> and put them into your own food.

---

> For a person is not able to be in truth  
> if the speech of the mouth is not like the thoughts of the heart,  
> And until the evil is gone from you,  
> Uqsa shall come and sting you in the arse.

> For this Uqsa — he is not silent,  
> for if he were silent he would be like one who is dying,  
> And even when he shall fall silent at the end of days,  
> I have put all of this inside an MP3000.

---

> So that ye may hear this again and again,  
> until ye do as ye say ye do,  
> Until the evil is gone from you and from the world —  
> Yours in love, the Sting of Stings.
