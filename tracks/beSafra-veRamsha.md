# beSafra, veRamsha

A spare, incantatory piece that draws the listener into a rhythm of remembrance at the junctures of the day: morning, evening, the middle of the night. With each awakening from sleep, Uqsa calls the listener to look to the sun or the moon and know that they stand in qiyyuma — existence itself, reality, endurance. There is no narrative here, no accusation; only a steady, mantra-like insistence that the everyday act of opening one's eyes is an encounter with the fundament of being. The song returns to its opening lines, turning contemplation into a cycle, a ritual, a grounding.

## בְּצַפְֿרָא, בְֿרַמְשָׁא

> בְּצַפְֿרָא, בְֿרַמְשָׁא וּבְֿפַֿלְגֿוּתָֿא דְֿלֵילְיָא  
> דאיתפתחו עיניכון בכל זמנא וזמנא

> וְתִֿנְדְֿעוּן דְּאַתּוּן קָיְמִין בְּקִיּוּמָא

### בבוקר, בערב

> בבוקר, בערב ובאמצע הלילה  
> [כ]שנפתחו עיניכם בכל פעם ופעם  
> תסתכלו לשמש או שמא לירח  
> ותדעו שאתם עומדים _(~נמצאים)_ בקיום.

> בבוקר, בערב ובאמצע הלילה  
> בזמן שאתם קמים מן המיטה  
> תזכרו את השמש או שמא את הירח  
> ותדעו שאתם עומדים בקיום.

### In the morning, in the evening

> In the morning, in the evening, and in the middle of the night,  
> When your eyes have opened time after time,  
> Ye shall look to the sun, or perhaps to the moon,  
> And ye shall know that ye are standing in existence _(~reality)_.

> In the morning, in the evening, and in the middle of the night,  
> At the time that ye rise from the bed,  
> Ye shall remember the sun, or perhaps the moon,  
> And ye shall know that ye are standing in existence.
