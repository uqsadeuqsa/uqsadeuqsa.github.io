## Pronunciation

### Vowels

* Pataḥ: Hungarian á (long a) - clear, open front vowel
* Qamaṣ: Hungarian a (short a) - mid-central vowel, slightly constricted, with Persian-influenced half-closed quality in final position
* Segol: Lowered mid-front vowel [ɛ] (similar to Hungarian/German 'ö')
* Shva: Central mid vowel [ə] (schwa) - exactly like Amharic shva, similar to Turkish/Romanian "î", not leaning toward "e" as in Levantine Arabic

### Gutturals & Throat Sounds

* Aleph (א): Glottal stop, as in "Africa"
* Hey (ה): Soft but distinct [h], stronger than Hungarian's weak aspiration
* Ḥet (ח): True guttural fricative [ħ] - "angry Arabic" quality, pronounced from the back of the throat
* Ayin (ע): Voiced pharyngeal fricative [ʕ] - even more emphatic and "deeper" than modern Arabic Ayin, preserving ancient aggressive guttural quality
* Qof (ק): Uvular stop [q] - from the back of the throat but still sounding like "c", not completely silent like Palestinian Arabic

### Begadkefet Letters

* Bet (ב): [b] with dagesh; without dagesh: soft bilabial fricative [β] like the soft "b" in Amharic "mevlat" (softer than typical [v])
* Gimel (ג): [g] with dagesh; without dagesh: guttural [ɣ] like Arabic ghayn ("punctured g" as in "garbie") - distinct from Yemeni [dʒ] pronunciation
* Dalet (ד): [d] with dagesh; without dagesh: [ð] as in "this"
* Kaf (כ): [k] with dagesh; without dagesh: vigorous uvular fricative [χ] ("thrilling khhhhhh") like Modern Hebrew but more pronounced
* Taw (ת): [t] with dagesh; without dagesh: [θ] as in "think"

### Emphatic Consonants

* Tet (ט): Emphatic plosive [tˤ] - "exploding t" with strong aspiration

### Sibilants

* Sin (שׂ): [s] - normal "s"
* Samech (ס): [t͡s] - single consonant like "z" in Italian "pizza" (not pits-tsa)
* Tzadi (צ): [sˤ] - emphatic hard "SS" sound (completely reversed from Modern Hebrew [t͡s])

### Semi-Vowels & Liquids

* Vav (ו): Labio-velar approximant [w] - soft "w" sound between [v] and [u], distinct from both Yemeni [u] and Modern Hebrew [v]
* Resh (ר): Alveolar trill [r] - deep, forward thrill like Romanian/Italian "R" (as in "dame un grrrr"), distinct from Yemeni back-of-throat pronunciation

### Special Features

* Gemination (Dagesh forte): Consonant lengthening fully realized
* Systematic distinction between guttural and frontal articulations
