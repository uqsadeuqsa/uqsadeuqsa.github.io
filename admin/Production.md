## Translation

English translations are as literal as possible while still reading as proper, modern English. The only archaic forms retained are those that disambiguate singular vs. plural in the second person, a distinction that modern English lost but that is grammatically significant in the Aramaic originals.

* Subject: plural ye is used when addressing a group; singular you when addressing an individual.
* Possessive: modern your / yours is normally used, but if a line’s meaning would be unclear without number, thy / thine (singular) vs. your / yours (plural) may be employed.
  No other artificial archaisms (thee, thou, verb endings, etc.) are introduced unless forced by this disambiguation rule.


## Vocal

Three distinct vocal modes convey different emotional states and character aspects: the **Natural Voice** serves as the foundational tone, the **Aggressive Voice** delivers confrontational content, while the two **Silly Voice** variations provide mocking commentary and ironic emphasis.

* **Natural Voice** - High Baritone / Low Tenor
* **Aggressive Voice** - Medium/High Baritone to Low Tenor
* **High Silly Voice** - High Baritone to Medium Tenor (nasal/staccato)
* **Low Silly Voice** - Medium Baritone (Jamaican-style ironic boasting)

The two silly voices serve distinct purposes: the High Silly Voice uses exaggerated nasal/staccato delivery for frantic, chaotic energy, while the Low Silly Voice employs Jamaican-style affectation to mockingly imitate empty self-importance and ironic boasting.
